ABOUT THE STANDARD

The open data movement has demonstrated its value to society on many occasions, particularly with recent efforts to improve information about transit systems. Following the example of Open311, this initiative proposes a specification for road event data that matches open data criteria and will also advocate for public organizations to publish traffic data. With Open511, developers and citizens can get the most out of your jursidictionâ€™s data by turning your road events into powerful and innovative software and analysis.

USE CASES

Improving Public Safety

Public services work better with better information. Open data standards like Open511 help jurisdic- tions maximize their communications offerings by ensuring their employees have all the data they need to do their job efficiently. For example, in North Vancouver, transportation data from the city and province is provided to firefighters within their trucks so they can get to emergency locations as fast as possible by avoiding traffic and construction. Since the roads they frequent are under different jurisidic- tions, its important to have data from different levels of government for a comprehensive view of road conditions. With multi-jurisidictional data sharing, public service and thus civil safety is improved.
